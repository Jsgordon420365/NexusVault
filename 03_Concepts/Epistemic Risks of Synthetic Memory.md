# **Epistemic Risks of Synthetic Memory**

When a language model is tasked with summarizing a complex project meeting or a dense research paper, it operates probabilistically. It may confidently omit a dissenting opinion, flatten a crucial nuance, or hallucinate a consensus that never existed.37 If that synthetic summary is canonicalized into the vault without a clear provenance marker, and is later retrieved by an autonomous agent to inform a critical project decision, the system has experienced epistemic drift—the permanent loss of original, verifiable truth. Over time, a vault polluted with unmarked synthetic summaries becomes an untrustworthy foundation for decision-making.
