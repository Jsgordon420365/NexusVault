# **Navigating Atomicity Limits**

The Zettelkasten principle of atomic notes—restricting one core idea per note—aligns perfectly with language model retrieval mechanisms.44 A vault composed of thousands of concise, highly focused atomic notes inherently provides clean, self-contained chunks for vector databases, bypassing the need for complex splitting algorithms. However, over-atomization presents a significant danger. If a complex concept is fractured across fifty single-sentence notes, the vector database may only retrieve a fragmented subset of those notes, severely degrading the model's ability to grasp the broader narrative. Notes must be authored to be large enough to contain a complete thought and its supporting evidence, yet small enough to cover a single, well-defined topic.
