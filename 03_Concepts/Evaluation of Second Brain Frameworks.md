# **Evaluation of Second Brain Frameworks**

| Framework | Core Mechanism | Human Utility | Machine (LLM) Utility |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **PARA** (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives) | Strict folder hierarchy based on actionability and current operational focus.9 | High. Excellent for operational focus, task management, and hiding inactive data from daily view. | Low. Folder paths provide minimal semantic context to vector databases unless explicitly injected as metadata.10 |
| **Zettelkasten** | Flat structure of atomic, highly linked, emergent notes categorized by idea rather than project.11 | Medium to High. Excellent for creative synthesis, but requires a high maintenance burden and cognitive discipline. | Very High. Atomic notes map perfectly to embedding chunks. Dense backlinks provide rich adjacency context for reasoning.13 |
| **MOCs** (Maps of Content) | Hub notes that index, outline, and contextualize a specific cluster of related notes.15 | High. Provides a curated, top-down entry point to navigate complex topics without feeling overwhelmed. | High. MOCs act as ideal context files that rapidly orient an AI agent at the beginning of a stateless session.16 |
| **Evergreen Notes** | Notes that evolve over time, moving from fleeting seeds to heavily refined, permanent concepts.17 | High. Encourages long-term knowledge compounding and prevents the accumulation of raw, unrefined data. | High. Provides high-signal, noise-free text for retrieval, ensuring models base their reasoning on verified user synthesis. |
